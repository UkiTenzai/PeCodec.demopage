Advancing Speech Codec with Multi-Scale Residual Networks and Auditory Deep Feature Losses

Overview

Recently, neural speech codecs have significantly surpassed traditional codecs in reconstructing high-definition speech at lower bitrates. However, existing neural speech codecs typically rely on basic residual networks and are primarily trained using specific time-domain or frequency-domain reconstruction loss functions. As a result, they fail to capture as much information as possible for reconstruction and do not adequately consider human auditory perception. In this paper, we propose a novel framework, which utilizes a multi-scale residual network to improve coding efficiency. Furthermore, we introduce a series of auditory deep feature losses, further improving the naturalness of the reconstructed speech. In our experiments, both subjective and objective evaluations demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed framework and deep feature losses. Specifically, our proposed method can achieve high-quality reconstructed speech at a bitrate of 1.5 kbps with a 24 kHz sample rate.
